Legal Foundations

1.1

Employer’s Duty of Care (Fürsorgepflicht)

VERIFIED: Constitutional & Statutory Basis
Article 33(4) Grundgesetz (Basic Law):
The duty of care is constitutionally mandated as an “established principle of professional civil
service.”
§ 78 Bundesbeamtengesetz (BBG):
“Der Dienstherr hat im Rahmen des Dienst- und Treueverhältnisses für das Wohl der Beamtinnen
und Beamten [...] zu sorgen. Er schützt die Beamtinnen und Beamten bei ihrer amtlichen Tätigkeit
und in ihrer Stellung.”
(The employer must ensure the welfare of civil servants [...]. He protects civil servants in their
official duties and in their position.)
§ 45 Beamtenstatusgesetz (BeamtStG):
Parallel provision for state-level civil servants.
Source: dbb beamtenbund; dejure.org § 78 BBG

1.2

Duty to Inform (Belehrungspflicht)

VERIFIED: Federal Administrative Court Doctrine
BVerwG (Bundesverwaltungsgericht) established doctrine:
“Andererseits obliegt dem Dienstherrn keine aus der beamtenrechtlichen Fürsorgepflicht abzuleitende allgemeine Pflicht zur Belehrung und Beratung über alle für den Beamten einschlägigen
Vorschriften, zumal über solche, deren Kenntnis sich der Beamte unschwer selbst verschaffen kann.”
However, enhanced duty applies when:
• The official is in a recognizable error of fact or law (Sach- oder Rechtsirrtum)
• Special circumstances exist that trigger an information duty
• The regulations are not easily accessible to the employee
Source: BVerwG case law; PUBLICUS Boorberg Verlag

Organisational Misconduct (Organisationsverschulden)
VERIFIED: Three Types of Organisational Liability
According to German school law doctrine, school leaders face liability for three types of organisational failure:
1. Auswahlverschulden (Selection Misconduct):
Delegating responsibilities to unsuitable staff members.
2. Anweisungsverschulden (Instruction Misconduct):
“Dieses liegt dann vor, wenn dienstliche Anweisungen fehlen oder lückenhaft sind. Das Gesetz
verlangt das Vorliegen einer Anleitung zu sachgerechter Arbeit.”
(This occurs when official instructions are missing or incomplete. The law requires guidance
for proper work.)
3. Überwachungsverschulden (Supervision Misconduct):
Lack of monitoring mechanisms to verify compliance.
Source: schulleiter.de/schulwissenplus.de – Rechtsarchiv Haftung

CRITICAL: Anweisungsverschulden Applies Here
If school leadership fails to provide clear instructions about KLE requirements to teachers,
this constitutes Anweisungsverschulden. The school leadership – not the uninformed
teacher – bears liability.

Liability Analysis: Missed Information

4.1

Burden of Proof

VERIFIED: BVerwG Doctrine on Burden of Proof
Beweislast (Burden of Proof ):
“Der Beamte trägt die materielle Beweislast für die Verletzung der Fürsorgepflicht [...]. Den
Dienstherrn trifft die materielle Beweislast dafür, dass den, dessen Verhalten er sich
zurechnen lassen muss, kein Verschulden trifft.”
Translation: The official bears the burden of proof for the breach of duty of care. The employer
bears the burden of proof that there was no fault.
Consequence: “Deshalb geht ein ‘non liquet’ in Bezug auf die Verschuldensfrage zulasten des
Dienstherrn.”
(Therefore, unresolved questions of fault work against the employer.)
Source: BVerwG case law compilation; PUBLICUS Boorberg

The School Must Prove:
1. The document was delivered to the teacher
2. The teacher was informed it was mandatory reading
3. Reasonable time was given to read and understand it
4. The importance of the content was communicated
If the school cannot prove these points → The teacher is NOT liable.
Legal basis: Burden of proof lies with employer per BVerwG doctrine.

4.2

Fault Standards

VERIFIED: Liability Only for Gross Negligence
Article 34 Grundgesetz + § 839 BGB:
“Grundsätzlich gilt, dass der Dienstherr für die Taten seines Bediensteten haftet. Nur bei ‘vorsätzlichem’ oder ‘grob fahrlässigem’ Handeln des Bediensteten kann der Dienstherr diesen in Regress
nehmen.”
Translation: The employer is generally liable for the acts of employees. Only for intentional or
grossly negligent conduct can the employer seek recourse from the employee.
Implication: A teacher who was not informed cannot be grossly negligent for not knowing.
Source: DBV.de – Lehrer Haftung; vlbs-bremen.de

“Bringschuld” vs. “Holschuld”

VERIFIED: Legal Definitions from § 269 BGB
Holschuld (Duty to Fetch):
“Holschuld ist ein Begriff aus dem Schuldrecht und bedeutet, dass Leistungs- und Erfolgsort beim
Schuldner liegt.” The creditor must fetch the performance from the debtor.
Bringschuld (Duty to Deliver):
“Bei der Bringschuld muss der Schuldner die Leistung am Wohnort seines Gläubigers bewirken.”
The debtor must deliver to the creditor.
Source: Wikipedia – Holschuld; frage.de; § 269 Abs. 2 BGB

The Teacher’s Defense
Valid Defense Statement
A teacher can credibly defend themselves if they can truthfully state:
“I was never told this document existed. I was never informed that I needed to
read it. I received no training or briefing on KLE requirements or the implications of my certification status for Q-phase teaching.”
Legal effect: If this statement is credible and the school cannot prove otherwise, liability
shifts entirely to school leadership per BVerwG burden of proof doctrine.

VERIFIED: Standard of Expected Knowledge
BVerwG doctrine on reasonable expectations:
“Von einem Beamten kann nämlich grundsätzlich keine bessere Rechtseinsicht erwartet werden, als
ein Kollegialgericht sie nach sorgfältiger Prüfung gewonnen hat.”
Translation: An official cannot be expected to have better legal insight than a collegiate court
would reach after careful examination.
Implication: If the requirement was unclear or not communicated, the teacher cannot be blamed
for not knowing.
Source: PUBLICUS Boorberg – Fürsorgepflicht Teil 3

KLE Sprechen: Specific Requirements
VERIFIED: APVO M-V Requirements
§ 17 Abs. 5 APVO M-V:
“In den modernen Fremdsprachen ist von jeder Schülerin und jedem Schüler mindestens eine komplexe Leistungsermittlung im Kompetenzbereich Sprechen abzulegen.”
Translation: In modern foreign languages, every student must complete at least one complex
performance assessment in the speaking competency area.
§ 22 Abs. 5 APVO M-V:
When speaking assessment is combined with listening comprehension: Listening 20%, Speaking
80%.
Timing: Must be completed during the qualification phase (Q1–Q3).
Source: Handreichung komplexe Leistungsermittlung Sprechen (bildung-mv.de); landesrechtmv.de
VERIFIED: Who May Conduct KLE
Facultas Docendi (Teaching Qualification):
“Bei der Fakultas (facultas docendi) handelt es sich um einen älteren Begriff für die Qualifikation
von Gymnasiallehrern. Diese Fakultas bedeutet eine Lehrberechtigung für ein bestimmtes Fach
am Gymnasium nach Ablage der wissenschaftlichen Prüfung.”
Implication: Teachers without Lehrbefugnis/Facultas in the subject cannot conduct official
examinations including KLE.
Source: Wikipedia – Lehrberechtigung; dewiki.de – Facultas Docendi

Final Note – Legally Verified
A teacher without proper teaching certification (Lehrbefugnis) who was assigned to teach a Q-phase course bears NO personal liability for:
• The school’s failure to conduct KLE assessments
• The mismatch between their qualifications and the assignment
• Regulatory non-compliance resulting from improper staffing
These are administrative decisions made above the teacher’s level and remain the
responsibility of school leadership.
Legal basis:
• Art. 34 GG – State liability for employee actions
• Anweisungsverschulden doctrine – Leadership responsible for incomplete instructions
• BVerwG burden of proof – Employer must prove lack of fault
• § 78 BBG / § 45 BeamtStG – Fürsorgepflicht includes proper assignment
