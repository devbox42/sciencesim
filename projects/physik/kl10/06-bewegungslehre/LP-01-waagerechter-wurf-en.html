Step 3: The Explanation

Principle of Superposition

In horizontal projectile motion, two independent motions are superimposed:

Direction Type of Motion Cause
Horizontal (x) Uniform (v = constant) No force in x-direction
Vertical (y) Uniformly accelerated (Free fall) Weight force F_G = m·g

Key insight: The horizontal velocity does not affect the fall time!

ℹ️ Note on Gravitational Acceleration g

Exact value: g = 9.81 m/s²

We calculate with: g ≈ 10 m/s²

Why?

  • Mental math becomes possible
  • Error only ~2% (acceptable for school problems)
  • Results become "nice" numbers

In exams: g = 10 m/s² unless otherwise stated.

Why do both balls land at the same time?

The thrown ball moves horizontally – but this motion has no effect on the vertical motion. The same force acts on both balls downward: gravity.

Both balls fall with the same acceleration g = 10 m/s² – that's why they land at the same time.

Step 4: Basic Formulas

Step 1: Calculate Time of Flight

The ball falls from height h – how long does it take?

t = √(2h / g)
t = time of flight [s] · h = height [m] · g = 10 m/s²
Example: Time of Flight

A ball is thrown from h = 20 m height.

t = √(2 · 20 m / 10 m/s²) t = √(40/10) s² t = √4 s² t = 2 s

Step 2: Calculate Horizontal Range

Horizontally, the ball moves uniformly with v₀.

sx = v₀ · t
s_x = range [m] · v₀ = initial velocity [m/s] · t = time of flight [s]
Example: Range

The ball has v₀ = 15 m/s and falls for t = 2 s.

s_x = v₀ · t s_x = 15 m/s · 2 s s_x = 30 m

Step 5: Advanced Formulas

Now it gets more interesting: At what velocity and at what angle does the ball hit the ground?

Step 3: Vertical Final Velocity

While falling, the ball accelerates continuously (free fall).

vy = g · t
v_y = vertical velocity at impact [m/s]

Step 4: Resultant Velocity (Pythagoras!)

At impact, the ball moves both horizontally (v_x) and vertically (v_y). The actual velocity is the combination of both:

vres = √(vx² + vy²)
Pythagoras! · v_x = v₀ (stays constant!) · v_y = g · t
Example: Resultant Velocity

v₀ = 30 m/s, h = 20 m (so t = 2 s)

v_x = v₀ = 30 m/s (constant!) v_y = g · t = 10 · 2 = 20 m/s v_res = √(30² + 20²) v_res = √(900 + 400) v_res = √1300 v_res ≈ 36.1 m/s (≈ 130 km/h!)

Step 5: Impact Angle (Trigonometry!)

At what angle to the horizontal does the ball hit?

tan(α) = vy / vx
α = impact angle · Calculator: α = arctan(v_y / v_x)
Example: Impact Angle

v_x = 30 m/s, v_y = 20 m/s

tan(α) = v_y / v_x = 20 / 30 = 0.667 α = arctan(0.667) α ≈ 33.7° ≈ 34°

⚠️ Model Limitations

Our calculation neglects:

  • Air resistance: In reality, air slows things down → shorter range
  • Wind: Can change the trajectory
  • Earth's curvature: Relevant for very large distances
  • Rotation: Ball spin can affect trajectory

For school problems and short distances, the model is very accurate!
